The Real Cost of Meetings in an AI-First World

Published on 2026-03-23

Last week I wrote ~14,400 lines of code. Solo. In 7 days. That's not a flex, it's a data point. And it changes the math on everything, including meetings.

At 200 lines/day, meetings cost you hours. At 2,000 lines/day, meetings cost you weeks.

The Multiplier Effect

When one engineer ships 200 lines, an 1-hour meeting costs them 10% of a workday. When AI lets one engineer ship 2,000 lines, that same meeting costs them a full day of output. The meeting didn't get more expensive. The velocity did.

Every hour in a meeting is no longer just an hour. It's 200 lines of code that won't exist. It's a feature that won't ship this sprint. It's momentum you won't get back.

Why Hierarchies Thin Out

Traditional org structure exists to coordinate humans who can't see everything. Managers align, directors synchronize, VPs prioritize. It's overhead to solve a coordination problem.

AI changes the math. One engineer with agents can see the whole codebase, own the whole stack, ship the whole feature. You don't need a layer of people to aggregate what one person can now hold in context.

Shallow hierarchy isn't a cultural choice, it's an economic one. When the cost of coordination (meetings, sync, hand-offs) exceeds the cost of duplication, you stop coordinating and start shipping solo.

The Trade-Off

This isn't without cost. Solo builders ship fast but drift fast. Without peer review, without cultural sync, you optimize for output over alignment. Eventually, you need humans for vision, for accountability, for the things machines can't hold.

The question isn't "meetings or no meetings." It's "what's worth coordinating, and what's worth letting fly solo?"

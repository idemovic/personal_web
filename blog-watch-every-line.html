Watch Every Line of AI Code. Is It Possible at 2K Lines/Day?

Published on 2026-03-17

The "read every line" rule is dying. It worked when engineers shipped 200 lines a day, but at 2,000 lines, powered by AI agents like Cursor or Claude, the old review process becomes the ultimate bottleneck.

High-Velocity Oversight

You cannot manually review 2,000 lines daily; that's a 12-hour second job. Instead of line-by-line validation, shift to Architectural Governance. Own the "boundaries" (APIs, data shapes, and auth) while delegating the "inner loops" to automated testing and strategic sampling.

Towards Architecture

Code is liability. While AI produces functional, well-commented blocks, its speed breeds overconfidence. The goal of review has shifted from syntax verification to risk management. You must act less like a proofreader and more like a Chief Architect.
